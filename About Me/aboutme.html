About Me

Hi! I am Joshua and I am currently a first year student studying Chemical Engineering at the University of Waterloo. The degree that I will have at the end of my undergraduate will be the "Bachelor of Applied Science".

Chemical Engineering

I study Chemical Engineering because of my interest in elements. I believe that a deep understanding of elements will allow us to create innovative, productive, and durable products as they can be thought of as the building blocks of our world.

I find the design of processes interesting as well. Being able to build a chemical process that brings in one type of material and outputs another is cool to me. This interest of mine has grown through the use of AutoCAD Plant 3D, which I practiced using in my chemical engineering design course. In the future, I plan to research more on P&IDs and how to effectively use AutoCAD Plant 3D.

Personal Interests

One of my interests is in the field of forensics science. I participated in HOSA, a biology contest, and competed as part of the forensics team. There, I learned many ways to identify people from the evidence and how to recreate crime scenes. Specifically, I found entomology in forensics extremely compelling.

My hobbies include playing badminton and basketball. I also enjoy watching anime and playing video games like most people do :)

Aspirations

As a chemical engineer, my goal is to create more effective and durable products. As we know, materials are finite so we have to conserve as much as we can. By creating such products, there would be fewer materials being burned and wasted, while at the same time, making these products more productive than their predecessors.
